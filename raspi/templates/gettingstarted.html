GETTING STARTED

Welcome to the animatronic eyes project! This guide will help you get started with setting up and using your animatronic eyes. Follow the steps below to assemble the components, configure the system, and begin controlling your animatronic eyes.

STEP 1: Printing

Begin by printing all the necessary 3D components for the animatronic eyes. Ensure you have the following parts:

  • Eye Mechanism
  • Eye Mechanism
  • Eye Mechanism
  • Eye Mechanism

Instructions:

  • Use high-quality filament to ensure durability.
  • Print each component with a layer height of 0.2mm, which is more than precise enough for this model.
  • Use PLA for all parts except for the eyes, which are printed in ABS for a more natural appearance.
  • Verify the dimensions and quality of each printed part.

STEP 2: Post-Processing

After printing the components, you need to perform post-processing to ensure they fit and function correctly.

Instructions:

  • The eyes can be sanded and smoothed as much as you like, but a minimum of sanding may be necessary.
  • Some holes are designed to be undersized for direct screwing, while others are oversized for a snug fit. If your printer makes holes too small to screw into or rotate smoothly, use a hand drill to drill out the holes for better precision.

STEP 3: Assembly

After printing the components, you need to perform post-processing to ensure they fit and function correctly.

Instructions:

  • Connect the two bases with 10mm/12mm M3 bolts. This pivot point is for the y-axis of the eye motion and the eyelids.
  • Place the servo in position and screw it in with some 4 or 6mm M2 screws. This serves as the actuator for the x-axis motion.
  • Attach the y-axis arm to the sub-base with a 4/5/6mm M3 screw, and attach a servo horn on the third hole from the center using a 4mm or 6mm M2 screw.
  • Start building the x-axis assembly by screwing the forks into the eye-adaptors with 4/5/6mm M3 bolts. The fork holes should be oversized so the screws bite into the adaptor; one goes in on a funny angle but you should be able to get it in.
  • Attach the three-point connector to the top of the forks; the M3 screw will bite into the undersized hole in the fork component. Also attach a servo arm on the final hole to the center of the three-point connector using a 5mm M3 bolt. The hole on the servo arm will likely need to be drilled to 2.5mm - 2.8mm to accept the screw. Manipulate the assembly regularly to ensure it all moves okay without friction as you build it up.
  • Attach the eye center-link to the eye adaptors with an 8mm M3 screw. Make sure the flat surface of the center-link is facing up and the sloping section facing down. You can also plug in the eyes at this stage.
  • Screw all this to the center of the sub-base with two 8/12mm M3 bolts.
  • Load up the servo block with 5 9G servos, in the correct orientation shown.
  • Work out which eyelid is which using the graphic, and connect the relevant connector with a 4mm or 6mm M2 screw. Attach a servo arm to the other end.
  • Attach the eyelids to the base, but don't worry about connecting any servo horns yet.

STEP 4: Wiring & Final Assembly

Now, upload the code to the Arduino and wire everything up. Ensure all servos are powered and in their neutral position, then link up all the servo arms to the servos with the eyes facing straight forward in a neutral position.

And all should be done!
