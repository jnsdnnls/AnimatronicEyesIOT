HOW DoES IT WORK?

This section provides an overview of how the animatronic eyes system operates. By understanding the interactions between the various components and the control logic, you can gain insight into the engineering behind the lifelike movements of the eyes.

System overview

The animatronic eyes system is a sophisticated assembly of electronic components designed to mimic natural eye movements. At its core, the system uses an ESP32 microcontroller to coordinate the actions of servo motors and other peripherals. Wireless communication allows for remote control, making the system both flexible and user-friendly.

Control and Communication

The ESP32 microcontroller serves as the brain of the system, processing inputs from the joystick and potentiometer to generate precise control signals. The PCA9685 PWM driver extends the microcontrollerâ€™s capabilities, allowing it to control multiple servos for synchronized eye and eyelid movements. Wi-Fi and Bluetooth connectivity enable wireless operation and integration with other devices.

Power Management

Reliable power is crucial for the animatronic eyes. The Li-Ion 18650 battery provides a steady power supply, while the USB-C TP4056 charging module ensures safe and efficient recharging. The system is designed to maximize battery life, allowing for prolonged use without frequent recharging.

Movement Mechanisms

The precise and smooth motion of the animatronic eyes is achieved through the use of 9G micro servo motors. These servos are controlled by the PCA9685 driver, which receives signals from the ESP32. The servos adjust the position of the eyes and eyelids in real time, based on user input and pre-programmed sequences.

User Interaction

The XY analog joystick and 10kOhm potentiometer provide intuitive control over the animatronic eyes. The joystick allows for easy adjustment of eye direction, while the potentiometer fine-tunes the eyelid movements. This setup enables users to create lifelike expressions and movements effortlessly.
